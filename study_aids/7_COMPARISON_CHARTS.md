# SYSTEM OF LAW COMPARISONS

## Chart 1: Continental vs. Anglo-Saxon Legal Systems

| Feature | CONTINENTAL (Civil Law) | ANGLO-SAXON (Common Law) |
|---------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Origin** | Roman law | English legal tradition |
| **Primary source** | Codified statutes & codes | Case law (judicial precedent) |
| **Judge's decisions** | Do NOT have force of law | **HAVE THE VALUE OF LAW** |
| **Legal reasoning** | Deductive (general → specific) | Inductive (specific → general) |
| **Codification** | Comprehensive codes | Fragmented, case-based |
| **Role of judges** | Apply existing law | Create law through precedent |
| **Flexibility** | Less flexible (code-bound) | More flexible (case-by-case) |
| **Examples** | France, Germany, Poland | UK, USA, Australia |

**EXAM TIP:** Remember "Anglo = Judges MAKE law"

---

## Chart 2: Monist vs. Dualist Systems

| Feature | MONIST | DUALIST |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Number of systems** | ONE unified system | TWO separate systems |
| **International law status** | Automatically part of domestic law | Must be transformed/incorporated |
| **Application** | Direct application possible | Requires implementing legislation |
| **Conflict resolution** | International may prevail | Domestic usually prevails |
| **Poland's approach** | Modified monist (int'l treaties > statutes) | - |

---

## Chart 3: Natural Law vs. Positive Law

| Feature | NATURAL LAW | POSITIVE LAW |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| **Source** | Nature, reason, morality, God | Human-made (parliament, government) |
| **Basis** | Universal moral principles | Legislative enactment |
| **Morality** | Inherently moral | Not necessarily moral |
| **Binding power** | Moral obligation | Legal obligation |
| **Courts bound?** | Not formally | Yes, formally |
| **Example** | Human dignity rights | Tax code |
| **Radbruch Formula** | Used to invalidate unjust positive law | Can be overridden if extremely unjust |

---

## Chart 4: Living Constitution vs. Originalism

| Feature | LIVING CONSTITUTION | ORIGINALISM |
|---------|--------------------| ------------|
| **Interpretation basis** | CURRENT circumstances | ORIGINAL intent of framers |
| **Constitution viewed as** | Evolving document | Fixed document |
| **Meaning** | Changes with society | Stays as originally meant |
| **Flexibility** | High | Low |
| **Critics say** | Judges make law | Ignores social progress |
| **Famous proponent** | - | Justice Antonin Scalia |

---

## Chart 5: Legal vs. Factual Presumption

| Feature | LEGAL PRESUMPTION | FACTUAL PRESUMPTION |
|---------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Source** | Required by LAW | Based on LOGIC |
| **Example** | Paternity within 300 days of divorce | "Smoking gun" evidence |
| **Can be rebutted?** | Yes | Yes |
| **Shifts burden of proof?** | Yes | Yes |
| **Created by** | Statute/legislation | Logical inference |

---

## Chart 6: Civil Law Method vs. Administrative Law Method

| Feature | CIVIL LAW METHOD | ADMINISTRATIVE LAW METHOD |
|---------|------------------|---------------------------|
| **Parties** | EQUAL (private vs private) | UNEQUAL (state vs individual) |
| **Interpretation** | **EXPANSIVE** allowed | **RESTRICTIVE** only |
| **Examples** | Contracts, property, family | Tax, criminal, permits |
| **Rationale** | Party autonomy | Protect individual from state |
| **Burden of proof** | On claiming party | On accusing authority |

# INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY COMPARISONS

## Chart 7: Moral Rights vs. Economic Rights (Copyright)

| Feature | MORAL RIGHTS | ECONOMIC RIGHTS |
|---------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Duration** | **FOREVER** (no time limit) | Life + 70 years |
| **Transferable?** | **NO** (inalienable) | Yes |
| **Content** | Attribution, integrity, first publication | Use, reproduce, sell, license |
| **After death** | Protected by family/successors | Transfer to heirs → public domain |
| **Waivable?** | No | Yes (can license) |
| **Purpose** | Protect personality/honor | Protect economic interests |

**EXAM TIP:** "Moral rights are like your name - you can't sell your identity"

| Type | Duration | Renewable? | Notes |

| **Industrial Design** | 25 years max | Yes (5-year terms) | Up to 5 renewals |
| **Utility Model** | 10 years | No | From filing date |
| **Copyright (economic)** | Life + 70 years | No | Automatic |
| **Copyright (moral)** | **Forever** | N/A | Inalienable |

## Chart 9: Patent vs. Trademark vs. Industrial Design

| Feature | PATENT | TRADEMARK | INDUSTRIAL DESIGN |

# COMPETITION LAW COMPARISONS

## Chart 10: Cartel vs. Parallel Behavior

| Feature | CARTEL | PARALLEL BEHAVIOR |
|---------|--------|-------------------|
| **Definition** | Coordinated action through agreement | Independent decisions, similar outcomes |
| **Cause** | Communication/coordination | Market transparency, similar costs |
| **Legal status** | **PROHIBITED** | **LEGAL** |
| **Evidence needed** | Proof of coordination | Market explanation sufficient |
| **Example** | Secret price-fixing meetings | All companies raise prices due to raw material costs |
| **Key case** | ETURAS | Wood Pulp II |
| **Burden of proof** | On authority (but accused must explain) | Accused must show market reasons |

**EXAM TIP:** "If market forces can explain it, it's not a cartel"

---

## Chart 11: Article 101 vs. Article 102 TFEU

| Feature | ARTICLE 101 (Cartels) | ARTICLE 102 (Abuse of Dominance) |
|---------|----------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Prohibits** | Agreements restricting competition | Abuse of dominant position |
| **Requires** | Agreement/concerted practice | Dominant position + abuse |
| **Number of parties** | 2+ undertakings | Can be single company |
| **Position prohibited?** | The agreement itself | Only the ABUSE, not dominance |
| **Exemptions** | Yes (Article 101(3), de minimis) | No general exemptions |
| **De minimis** | H: <5%, V: <10% | No de minimis |

---

## Chart 12: Horizontal vs. Vertical Agreements

| Feature | HORIZONTAL | VERTICAL |
|---------|------------|----------|
| **Parties** | Competitors (same level) | Different levels (supplier-distributor) |
| **Risk level** | Higher risk | Lower risk |
| **De minimis threshold** | < 5% market share | < 10% market share |
| **Examples** | Price-fixing between rivals | Exclusive distribution, resale price |
| **Treatment** | More strictly scrutinized | Often allowed (efficiency gains) |

---

## Chart 13: Types of Abuse of Dominant Position

| Abuse Type | Description | Example |
|------------|-------------|---------|
| **Unfair pricing** | Excessive or predatory prices | Below-cost selling to kill competitors |
| **Limiting production** | Restricting output to harm consumers | Reducing supply to raise prices |
| **Discrimination** | Different terms for equivalent transactions | Charging different prices without justification |
| **Tied sales** | Requiring purchase of unrelated products | Microsoft + Internet Explorer |
| **Refusal to deal** | Refusing to supply competitors | Not licensing essential patents |
| **Loyalty rebates** | Rebates that foreclose competitors | British Airways commission scheme |
| **Self-preferencing** | Favoring own products/services | Google Shopping case |

# COMPANY LAW COMPARISONS

## Chart 17: Entrepreneur vs. Enterprise

| Feature | ENTREPRENEUR | ENTERPRISE |
|---------|--------------|------------|
| **Nature** | PERSON (subject) | PROPERTY (object) |
| **Definition** | Natural/legal person conducting business | Organized set of assets for business |
| **Legal status** | Has legal capacity | Collection of rights and things |
| **Components** | The who | The what (equipment, IP, contracts, etc.) |

---

## Chart 18: Sole Proprietorship vs. Joint-Stock Company

| Feature | SOLE PROPRIETORSHIP | JOINT-STOCK COMPANY |
|---------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Legal separation** | NO (owner = business) | YES (separate legal person) |
| **Liability** | **UNLIMITED** (all personal assets) | **LIMITED** (share value only) |
| **Minimum capital** | None | 100,000 PLN |
| **Registration** | CEIDG | KRS |
| **Governance** | Owner decides all | Board structure required |
| **Creditor claims** | Personal property at risk | Only company assets |
| **Complexity** | Simple | Complex |

---

|------|-----------|--------------|--------------|-------------|
| **Sole Proprietor** | Unlimited | None | CEIDG | Simplest form |
| **Civil Partnership** | Unlimited | None | CEIDG (each partner) | Partners = entrepreneurs |
| **General Partnership** | Unlimited | None | KRS | All liable |
| **Professional Partnership** | Limited (not for others' mistakes) | None | KRS | For professions |
| **Limited Partnership** | Mixed | None | KRS | General + limited partners |
