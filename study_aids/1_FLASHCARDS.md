# SECTION 1: THE SYSTEM OF LAW

---

## Card 1
**Q: In which legal system do decisions made by judges have the value of law?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A: The ANGLO-SAXON (Common Law/English) legal system** - through the doctrine of precedent (stare decisis). In the Continental (Civil Law) system, judge decisions do NOT have the force of law.

</details>

---

## Card 2
**Q: What is the opposite of positive law in constitutional interpretation?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A: The LIVING CONSTITUTION concept** - which allows interpreters to derive meaning from CURRENT circumstances, rather than the original intent of the lawmakers.

</details>

---

## Card 3
**Q: What is the difference between a MONIST and DUALIST legal system?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A:**
- **MONIST**: International and domestic law form ONE system; international law automatically applies
- **DUALIST**: Two SEPARATE systems; international law must be transformed into domestic law to apply

</details>

---

## Card 4
**Q: What is the status of international agreements in the Polish Constitution compared to acts of Parliament?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A: International agreements ratified with parliamentary consent have PRIORITY BEFORE STATUTES** (acts of Parliament). The Constitution itself remains supreme.

</details>

---

## Card 5
**Q: Which branch of law was influenced by French law in Poland - Contract law (private) or Administrative law (public)?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A: CONTRACT LAW (Private Law)** - including Civil Code structure, contract formation rules, and property law concepts.

</details>

---

## Card 6
**Q: What does "Lex retro non agit" mean?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A: "The law does not act retroactively"** - A person cannot be punished for an act committed BEFORE the law was enacted.

</details>

---

## Card 7
**Q: What is the difference between a LEGAL presumption and a FACTUAL presumption?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A:**
- **LEGAL presumption**: Required by LAW; shifts burden of proof (e.g., paternity presumption within 300 days of divorce)
- **FACTUAL presumption**: Based on LOGIC and evidence (e.g., "smoking gun")

Both can be rebutted by proof to the contrary.

</details>

---

## Card 8
**Q: Which interpretation method is allowed in Civil Law vs Administrative Law?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A:**
- **Civil Law**: EXPANSIVE interpretation allowed (equality of parties)
- **Administrative Law**: Only RESTRICTIVE interpretation (state vs individual)

</details>

# SECTION 2: COPYRIGHT LAW

---

## Card 9
**Q: When is a work "established" for copyright purposes?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A: When it takes a RECOGNIZABLE FORM** - even if incomplete. No formalities required. Example: An unfinished thesis with identifiable creative content is already protected.

</details>

---

## Card 10
**Q: What is the "exhaustion of rights" (first sale doctrine)?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A: A copyright owner's right to control copies EXHAUSTS on the FIRST SALE** with their consent. After first sale, the owner cannot prevent resale of legitimately purchased items.

</details>

---

## Card 11
**Q: What is the duration of MORAL rights vs ECONOMIC rights in copyright?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A:**
- **MORAL rights**: NO TIME LIMIT (perpetual)
- **ECONOMIC rights**: Life of author + 70 years

</details>

---

## Card 12
**Q: Which copyright rights are NOT transferable?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A: MORAL RIGHTS are INALIENABLE** - cannot be sold, given away, or transferred. They include: right to authorship, integrity of work, and first publication.

</details>

---

## Card 13
**Q: How are Xerox operators affected by copyright law?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A: Must pay fees up to 3% OF PROCEEDS to collective management organizations. Proceeds distributed 50/50 between authors and publishers.**

</details>

---

## Card 14
**Q: If a work is in the public domain, are you free to use it WITHOUT indicating the source?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A: NO! You must still CREDIT THE AUTHOR.** Moral rights (including attribution) persist even after economic rights expire.

</details>

---

## Card 15
**Q: Whose consent is needed to publish a letter?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A: Consent needed from:**
1. **The AUTHOR** (writer) - for copyright
2. **The RECIPIENT** - for privacy rights
3. **Heirs** if author is deceased

</details>

# SECTION 4: CARTELS

---

## Card 25
**Q: What is a CARTEL?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A: An AGREEMENT between undertakings, decisions by associations, or CONCERTED PRACTICES that have as their object or effect the prevention, restriction, or distortion of COMPETITION.**

</details>

---

## Card 26
**Q: What are the possible FORMS of a cartel?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A:**
1. Written agreements
2. Oral agreements
3. Informal statements
4. Concerted practices
5. Information exchange
6. Trade association decisions

</details>

---

## Card 27
**Q: Can you enter into a cartel WITHOUT saying or writing anything?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A: YES! Through:**
- Concerted practices
- Tacit collusion
- Information exchange
- Algorithmic coordination
(ETURAS case - coordination through booking system without direct communication)

</details>

---

## Card 28
**Q: What is the difference between a CARTEL and PARALLEL BEHAVIOR?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A:**
- **CARTEL**: Coordinated action through agreement - PROHIBITED
- **PARALLEL BEHAVIOR**: Independent decisions leading to similar outcomes (due to market conditions) - LEGAL

(Wood Pulp II case, 1993)

</details>

---

## Card 29
**Q: When is a SELECTIVE DISTRIBUTION agreement NOT a cartel?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A: When:**
1. Goods need specialized service (electronics, watches)
2. Luxury goods requiring brand protection
3. Criteria are OPEN, TRANSPARENT, NON-DISCRIMINATORY
4. Restrictions necessary to maintain prestige (L'Oreal case)

</details>

---

## Card 30
**Q: What is the DE MINIMIS exemption for cartels?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A:**
- **HORIZONTAL cartels**: < 5% market share
- **VERTICAL agreements**: < 10% market share

Does NOT apply to price-fixing or market-sharing!

</details>

---

## Card 31
**Q: Is HAVING a dominant position prohibited?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A: NO! Having a dominant position is NOT prohibited. Only the ABUSE of it is prohibited** (Article 102 TFEU).

</details>

---

## Card 32
**Q: Does having 40% market share give CERTAINTY or only a PRESUMPTION of dominance?**

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**A: Only a PRESUMPTION of dominance** - not certainty! Actual research of market forces must be conducted.

</details>

<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

3. **LEVERAGING dominance** from search to shopping

</details>
